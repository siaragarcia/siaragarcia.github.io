From Then to Now...

By Siara Garcia
Ever since I was in grade school, I enjoyed working on my computer. Before doing an assignment, I used to ask my teachers if I could make the project into a PowerPoint presentation or type up the essay to turn it in. Now, virtually every assignment I turn in is via computer and keyboard--which, don't get me wrong, really excites me.  My interests in kindergarten weren't very orthodox compared to my peers. I loved Star Wars, and still do! Even though our technology isn't as advanced as it is in the films, the movies opened my eyes to the fact that we had great tech! As I grew up, my family got one computer and we all shared it. However, I used it the most--learning the different ways to complete everyday task and whatever I needed for school.

Shortly after realizing that, I had gotten a smart-phone. Almost as soon as i got it, I downloaded any apps that had to do with editing(at the time, only the free ones). I kept editing pictures, blending them to make two completely different pictures look like they belong in one, and drawing on my phone too. A while ago, I discovered anime and manga which I fell in love with. The art style is my favorite, the stories and plot twists are surprising (depending on which you watch) so then i started drawing. Digital drawing and traditional drawing are both passions of mine, and as programs get updates and new computers and drawing tablets are released, I wlill continue to draw. 

When I was just 2 years old, I would watch the news, whatever it was, in English and Spanish.  After turning 3, I could speak the basics of both languages. Most adults say television rots your brains, but it actually taught me to read and speak.  After learning how to read, I developed a love for literature and poetry, and kept finding new ways to write. To this day, I write stories and hope to write a screenplay or a novel one day. They say that writing goes hand in hand with drawing, and that to be skilled at both would allow you to show others exactly how you visioned your characters. That is my goal, to create a story in writing and show others my vision through drawings.

Might be hobbies or school-work, or maybe just me fooling around.
